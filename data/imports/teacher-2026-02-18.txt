A departure from what is normal, usual, or expected (often unwelcome).
Example: The sudden warm weather in January was an aberration in this normally frigid climate.

Logical / Conjunctive / Viewpoint (linking ideas, expressing certainty or appearance)
Accordingly (в соответствии с) — in a way that is appropriate to the situation
The rules changed; accordingly, we adapted. > Please act accordingly.
> The policy changed, and we updated the settings accordingly.

Consequently — as a result
He was late; consequently, he missed the start.
> The server ran out of memory. Consequently, the process crashed.
> He missed the deadline; consequently, the release was delayed.

Inevitably — in a way that cannot be avoided
Arguments inevitably arise in such discussions. > If you push that change today, you’ll inevitably break something.

Undoubtedly — (несомненно) without doubt; certainly
She is undoubtedly the best candidate.
> This is undoubtedly a big step forward.

Unequivocally — (equivocal = двусмысленный -> +un = однозначно) in a way that leaves no room for doubt

> The data unequivocally shows a decline.

Ostensibly — (по видимости) apparently or outwardly (but perhaps not really)
Ostensibly for health reasons, he quit.
> He left early, ostensibly because he was sick.

Seemingly — (по-видимому) appearing to be the case
Seemingly simple problems can be complex.
> Seemingly, everything was fine.
> He gave a seemingly honest answer.

Paradoxically — in a way that seems contradictory but may be true
Paradoxically, more choice can lead to less happiness.
> Paradoxically, doing less improved the result.

Ironically — in a way opposite to what is expected

> Ironically, I started sleeping better after I stopped worrying about sleep.
