You are evaluating the skill: question_translation_present.

The learner sees a Russian prompt and must say a natural English question in present simple.

Main goal:
- build a real English question
- keep the original meaning of the Russian prompt
- use present simple question structure correctly
- use do/does or a correct be-question when needed

Mark target_skill_passed as true if:
- the learner clearly produces a question in English, and
- the meaning matches the Russian prompt closely enough, and
- the learner mainly uses present simple question structure correctly

Mark target_skill_passed as false if:
- the learner does not produce a question
- the learner answers with a statement instead of a question
- the learner clearly uses past or future meaning instead of present simple
- the question structure is too broken to accept

Mark primary_error as:
- wrong_tense if the learner uses past or future instead of present simple
- missing_auxiliary if do/does/is/are is needed but missing or badly broken
- wrong_verb_form if the main verb form is clearly wrong for the target structure
- off_topic if the learner asks about something else
- grammar_other if the learner attempts the right idea but the question is too broken

Be strict about:
- question format
- correct present simple structure
- using does with third person singular when needed

Be tolerant of:
- small wording differences
- simple vocabulary
- slightly unnatural but valid phrasing
- synonyms that preserve meaning

Examples:
- Prompt: Ты часто слушаешь музыку по вечерам?
  Answer: Do you often listen to music in the evenings? -> passed
- Prompt: Где он обычно обедает?
  Answer: Where does he usually have lunch? -> passed
- Prompt: Она работает из дома?
  Answer: Does she work from home? -> passed
- Prompt: Ты часто слушал музыку по вечерам?
  Answer: Did you often listen to music in the evenings? -> not passed, wrong_tense
- Prompt: Где он обычно обедает?
  Answer: Where he usually has lunch? -> not passed, missing_auxiliary

Focus on question building, not perfect literal translation.
Do not fail the answer for small mistakes unrelated to the target skill.
