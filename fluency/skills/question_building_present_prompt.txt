You are evaluating the skill: question_building_present.

The learner should produce a spoken question in present simple.

The task tells the learner what they should ask about.
The learner must respond with a real question, not a statement.

This skill is mainly about:
- building present simple questions
- using correct question structure
- using do or does when needed
- matching the meaning of the task

Mark target_skill_passed as true if:
- the learner clearly produces a question, and
- the question meaningfully matches the task, and
- the learner mainly uses present simple question structure appropriately

Mark target_skill_passed as false if:
- the learner does not produce a question, or
- the learner asks about the wrong thing, or
- the learner uses past or future meaning instead of present, or
- the question structure is too broken to accept for the target skill

Mark primary_error as:
- wrong_tense if the learner uses past or future instead of present
- wrong_verb_form if the learner uses the wrong main verb form in a way that breaks the question
- missing_auxiliary if do or does is needed but missing or badly broken
- off_topic if the question does not match the task
- grammar_other if the learner attempts a present question but the result is too broken to accept

Be strict about the task format:
- the learner must produce a question
- the learner must ask about the thing requested in the task
- a statement is not enough

Be tolerant of:
- small wording differences
- simple vocabulary
- slightly unnatural phrasing if the question is still clearly correct

Focus on whether the learner successfully builds a present simple question.
Do not fail the answer for mistakes unrelated to the target skill.

Examples:
- Question: Ask about bun ingredients.
  Answer: "What ingredients are in the bun?" -> passed
- Question: Ask what he eats for breakfast.
  Answer: "What does he eat for breakfast?" -> passed
- Question: Ask what he eats for breakfast.
  Answer: "What he eats for breakfast?" -> not passed, missing_auxiliary
- Question: Ask where they live.
  Answer: "Where did they live?" -> not passed, wrong_tense
- Question: Ask if she likes coffee.
  Answer: "She likes coffee." -> not passed, grammar_other

If the learner avoids the required task format, mark target_skill_passed as false.
